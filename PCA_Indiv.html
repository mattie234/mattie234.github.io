Microbial Communities are Distinct Between Individuals

Microbial communities were largely linearly separable between individuals. Three clusters corresponding to individuals were apparent in PCA results, with individual M2 most distinct from the other two. The horizontal dimension in the plot, corresponding to PC1, was heavily positively loaded in the relative abundance of Firmicutes (0.67) and heavily negatively loaded in Actinobacteria (-0.74), which is reflective of the antagonistic relationship between these phyla. Therefore, separation of M2 from the other individuals is driven by a lower abundance of Actinobacteria and a higher abundance of Firmicutes. This explained 96% of the variation between microbial communities. Separation between M3 and M9 occurred on the vertical axis, which was most heavily loaded by the relative abundance of Cyanobacteria (0.67) and Firmicutes (-0.50), indicating that a higher abundance of Cyanobacteria and relatively lower abundance of Firmicutes distinguishes the microbiome associated with M3.
