Bacteroidetes and Firmicutes are antagonistic to Actinobacteria

In order to determine if Phylum-level signals were present that could be discriminated by PCA, pairwise relationships between individual phyla were explored using Pearson correlation coefficients and visualized in a heatmap. The relative abundance of Actinobacteria was strongly negative correlated with the relative abundances of both Bacteroidetes (r = -0.72) and Firmicutes (r = -0.97). Relative abundances of Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes demonstrated a strong positive relationship (r = 0.69). Similar relationships have been demonstrated in the oral microbiome, with Bacteroidetes and Firmicutes forming an ecological guild that is antagonistic to Actinobacteria [4].
