Percent Change

Compartative View of Stock Price Fluctations Using Percentage Change

Our second visualization utilizes the same data as our first visualization [1], but provides a more normalized and readily comparable view of how industries were affected by COVID-19. In order to visualize the effects of the pandemic, we plotted the monthly percentage change from the beginning of 2019 to February 2021. We created this visualization to be interactive and chose Tableau as the platform to do this. Tooltips include the date, the industry, and the number of recorded COVID cases in the US on that date. Furthermore, the viewer can select a single line in the legend to see one industry specifically. Because our other visualizations utilize subplots, we thought it would be helpful for ease of comparison to have overlapping lines that use a normalized measure of assessing change.

As mentioned before, the technology industry appeared to fluctuate the least throughout the course of the pandemic. Conversely, the airline industry fluctuated the most both positively and negatively with deep dips and high percentage growth after the pandemic began to fall more under control/the economy began reopening. The hotel industry also experienced high fluctuations that were comparable to the airline industry which may be because they attract a similar set of customers. Finally, something interesting on this visualization is that there were still a decent number of cases when some industries experienced positive growth. This trend indicates that reopening the economy may not have coincided with a significant reduction in caseloads.
